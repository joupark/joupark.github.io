Research Areas

What We Work On

01

Speech Enhancement

Recovering clean, intelligible speech from noisy and reverberant environments. We develop deep neural network architectures — including spectral masking and waveform-domain models — targeting real-world communication and assistive listening applications.

Deep Learning STFT / Masking Noise Cancellation PyTorch
02

UAV Control

Autonomous flight control for unmanned aerial vehicles in dynamic environments. Research spans sensor fusion, state estimation, and adaptive control — enabling stable UAV operation and smart transportation applications beyond line-of-sight.

ROS Adaptive Control IMU Fusion C++ / MATLAB
03

Vision-Language-Action

End-to-end models that ground natural language in visual perception to drive robot actions. We study cross-modal alignment, generalizable policy learning, and efficient architectures for embodied AI in open-world settings.

Transformer CLIP / LLM Embodied AI RL
04

Multimodal Perception

Unified perception frameworks fusing visual, auditory, and linguistic signals for scene understanding. Includes 360° video streaming, volumetric media, semantic-aware view prediction, and cross-modal attention for heterogeneous sensor streams.

360° Video Cross-modal Attention ViT HuggingFace

Principal Investigator

Dr. Jounsup Park leads the SAIL Lab at California Baptist University. His research spans the full arc from classical signal processing to modern AI — including speech enhancement, UAV autonomy, 360° video streaming, volumetric media, and multimodal perception.

He has published extensively at IEEE GLOBECOM, ACM Multimedia, SPIE, and in IEEE Transactions journals, and holds multiple US patents in wireless communications and signal processing. Prior to CBU, he conducted research at Microsoft Research and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.
